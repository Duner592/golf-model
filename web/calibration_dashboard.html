Calibration Dashboard

Beta

Do model probabilities happen as often as forecast?

New for the 2026 season. Calibration tracking is a beta initiative that started with the beginning of the 2026 archive. Treat the early read as directional while more completed tournaments build the sample.

This dashboard uses completed 2026 archive events and compares player-level model probabilities with actual outcomes. Lower Brier score and log loss are better. A positive bucket gap means the outcome happened more often than the model predicted.

How this page works

What it is checking: every player prediction from completed 2026 archive events is matched to the actual result. The page asks whether probabilities like 5%, 20%, or 70% happen roughly that often.

Metric selector: Win checks tournament winners, Top 10 checks actual top-10 finishes, and Make Cut checks whether the player made the cut where cut data can be inferred.

Calibration curve: the dotted diagonal is perfect calibration. Points above it mean the outcome happened more often than forecast; points below it mean the model forecast was too high for that bucket.

Buckets: rows are sorted by model probability and split into up to 10 similarly sized groups from lowest probability to highest. Each chart point and table row is one of those groups.

Avg Pred vs Actual: Avg Pred is the average model probability inside the bucket. Actual is the percentage of players in that same bucket who achieved the selected outcome.

Scores: Brier and log loss are player-level error scores where lower is better. Avg Gap is the average bucket miss in percentage points, weighted by rows.
